# WORLD WAR III
# PROJECT RULES
Version 2.0 — updated to match the "World War III 2100" reference manual
(phases, citizen/cyber systems, per-unit rules, starting deployment).

---

# 1. GAME OVERVIEW

World War III is a turn-based grand strategy game inspired by Axis & Allies.

Players command modern military alliances, conquer territories, develop their economy, launch missile strikes and use cyber warfare to dominate the world.

The game is entirely turn based.

No real-time gameplay exists.

Every action is deterministic except dice rolls.

---

# 2. OBJECTIVE

Victory is achieved by capturing regions marked with a white star.

A TEAM (an alliance of factions) wins by jointly capturing 15 white-star regions.

A SINGLE faction wins by capturing 7 white-star regions on its own.

Which regions carry a white star is data-driven (see data/countries.json,
Region.isVictoryStar) — never hardcoded.

CURRENT IMPLEMENTATION STATUS: 19 of the ~24 white stars visible on the map
artwork are marked (identified via pixel analysis of assets/maps/world-map.png,
cross-referenced against each region's known position). A handful in the
dense North Arabia/Center Arabia/Himalayas cluster couldn't be pinned to a
specific region with confidence and were left unmarked rather than guessed
— flag any region that visibly has a star on the map but isn't triggering
victory-star behavior, and it can be added with a one-line countries.json
edit (`"isVictoryStar": true`).

Team alliances (PROJECT_RULES.md section 2's "TEAM"): team-west = EUTO,
USA, SEATO; team-east = Russia, China, Arabia League (Faction.teamId,
data/factions.json).

## NEUTRAL COUNTRIES

Every neutral (unowned) region starts the game with a standing army — 3
Infantry, 2 Tanks, and 1 Helicopter (data/economy.json "neutralArmy") —
defending it. This is a real, data-driven army, not an abstract difficulty
modifier: attacking a neutral region means fighting these actual units
(section 8), same as attacking an enemy-owned region.

If a neutral region is captured politically (Political Influence, section
6), this army defects and joins the capturing player, immediately available.

If a neutral region is captured by force, the capturing player's Citizen
marker immediately drops by 5 places (captureSatisfactionPenaltyAttacker,
section 5) — same penalty as capturing an enemy region by force.

---

# 3. GAME FLOW

Every player completes all phases before the next player begins.

Turn order:

1. Buy Units Phase
2. Cyber Attack Phase
3. Attack Moves Phase
4. Attack Phase
5. Tactical Moves Phase
6. Place New Units Phase
7. Collect Income Phase

Income is collected LAST, at the end of the turn — it funds next turn's Buy Units
Phase, not this one.

---

# 4. BUY UNITS PHASE

The player may purchase:

- Units
- Missiles
- Buildings
- Technologies (future)

Purchased units enter the player's Reserve; they are not on the map yet.

The player may also spend money here on Citizen / Public Spending (section 5).

Money cannot become negative (section 27).

---

# 5. CITIZEN / PUBLIC SPENDING

Each faction has a Citizen Satisfaction marker on a 1-80 track (see
data/economy.json citizenSatisfactionMin/Max), starting at 30. The track is
divided into six data-driven zones (data/economy.json
citizenSatisfactionZones), lowest to highest:

- 1-20, red "rebellion" zone — see below.
- 21-40, black "neutral" zone — no effect.
- 41-50, gold zone — +2 income to treasury per turn.
- 51-60, gold zone — +3 income to treasury per turn.
- 61-70, gray zone — +2 victory points per turn.
- 71-80, gray zone — +2 victory points per turn.

The marker drops by 5 places automatically at the start of each of the
player's turns (EndTurnCommand), before that turn's income is credited.

If the resulting value falls in the red zone (<=20), the player's
rebellionLevel escalates by 1 (capped at 3) that turn:

- Level 1: no effect yet.
- Level 2 (and 3): that turn's income (including any zone bonus above) is
  halved.
- Level 3: the moment it is FIRST reached, the rebel army from
  data/economy.json's "rebelArmy" list (3 Infantry + 2 Tanks) spawns at the
  player's capital, if they still control it. This only spawns once per
  escalation to level 3, not every subsequent turn spent there.

CURRENT IMPLEMENTATION STATUS: the rebel army spawns as real units (owned by
a synthetic "rebels" id, never a real faction/player), but whether they
actually take the capital is not resolved — same scope boundary as section 8
(Attack Phase): full combat resolution against defended regions is future
work, not faked here.

During the Buy Units Phase, the player may spend money to raise their own
Citizen marker back up: 1 money = 1 point (data/economy.json has no separate
cost table — it's a flat rate), up to the track's max of 80. Since the
marker decays by 5 every turn, spending exactly 5 offsets that turn's decay
with no net change; spending more builds a buffer against future drops
(rebellion decay, or losing a region by force — see below). Once the marker
crosses back above 20 this way, rebellionLevel resets to 0.

Whenever a region is captured by force (not by political hacking):

- The capturing player's Citizen marker drops by 5
  (captureSatisfactionPenaltyAttacker, data/economy.json).
- The previous owner's Citizen marker drops by 2
  (captureSatisfactionPenaltyDefender, data/economy.json) — losing a home
  region also hurts morale, not just conquering one.

# 6. CYBER ATTACK PHASE

During the Cyber Attack Phase, the player may spend a flat
economyConfig.cyberAttackCost (data/economy.json, 3 by default) to attempt
ONE Cyber Attack action — Hacking or Political Influence, not one of each.
The cost is paid whether the attempt succeeds or fails; each is one d6
roll. This is enforced by PlayerState.hasUsedCyberAttackThisTurn, which
both HackCommand and PoliticalInfluenceCommand check and set, and which
resets when the player's own Cyber Attack Phase comes around again next
round (AdvancePhaseCommand) — not just documented behavior.

## HACKING

Target an enemy player, then roll a d6. If the roll is less than or equal
to the target's Hack Level (tracked per player, starts at 1), the attempt
succeeds: the target rolls a d6 too, and that many money transfers
straight from their treasury to the attacker's (clamped to whatever the
target actually has — you can never take more than they own).

## UPGRADE HACK LEVEL

Instead of Hacking or Political Influence, the player may spend
economyConfig.hackLevelUpgradeCost (10 by default) to raise their own Hack
Level by 1, up to economyConfig.hackLevelMax (4 by default). This is the
same Cyber Attack action pool — one of Hacking, Political Influence, or
Upgrade Hack Level per turn, not a separate allowance.

## POLITICAL INFLUENCE

Target a neutral (unowned) region, then roll a d6. If the result is less
than or equal to economyConfig.politicalInfluenceThreshold (3 by default),
the attacker places one influence token there.

A neutral region becomes the attacker's once they hold at least
economyConfig.politicalInfluenceMajority (2 by default) more influence
tokens there than any other faction. Capturing a region this way (political
takeover, not force) does not drop the Citizen marker (contrast section 5's
force-capture penalties), and any units already garrisoned there — every
neutral region has one, see section 2 — defect to the new owner
immediately.

Placed tokens are visible, not just tracked internally: the region detail
bar shows a per-faction token count (Region.influenceTokens), and the map
itself draws a small colored, numbered dot per faction next to the flag of
any region with tokens on it.

CURRENT IMPLEMENTATION STATUS: "available starting their next turn" for
defecting units isn't enforced separately — they get the new owner
immediately with whatever movesRemaining they already had.

# 7. ATTACK MOVES PHASE

This is the "combat move": the ONLY thing a unit does here is move INTO an
enemy (or garrisoned-neutral) region to attack it. Attack Moves is
ATTACK-ONLY — you cannot make a plain move into your own territory during
this phase (repositioning within your own lines is Tactical Moves,
section 17). Combat itself belongs to the following Attack Phase (section 8).

Units may move according to their Movement value (section 26). A unit may
never exceed its movement allowance, and movement is multi-hop: the range
is the movement value, not a single step. A Fighter (movement 4) can reach
a target up to 4 regions away, a Helicopter (movement 3) up to 3; the
attack consumes as many movement points as the path is long.

Pathing rules for reaching a target:
- Land units path through friendly/empty regions and enter the hostile
  region on the final hop (they cannot pass through hostile territory, and
  may cross a strait bridge only if they own both sides — data/straits.json).
- Aircraft ignore terrain: they fly over anything (friendly, hostile, sea)
  within their movement range.
- Naval units move through sea zones (data/sea-zones.json); naval combat is
  not modelled yet, so they have no attack targets.

On the map, dragging a unit onto an ATTACK target (red highlight) attacks
it. Only units that can reach a hostile region this phase are outlined in
gold as movable — an interior unit with no reachable enemy has nothing to
do during Attack Moves.

---

# 8. ATTACK PHASE

The player declares all attacks using units positioned during Attack Moves.

Missile attacks resolve FIRST, before any other combat.

For every attack:

Select source region.

Select target region.

Missiles are declared and resolve immediately (section 15).

Then remaining combat resolves per the Combat Order (section 10).

CURRENT IMPLEMENTATION STATUS: attacks are declared by moving a unit into
the target region during the Attack Moves Phase (section 7) — the
AttackCommand, dispatched by dragging a unit onto an enemy region or via
the movement panel's Attack action, both valid only during Attack Moves.
Land/air only — naval combat is not implemented, so naval units have zero
attack targets. Two outcomes:

- UNDEFENDED target: the region is captured immediately, the attacking unit
  occupies it, and the by-force Citizen Satisfaction penalties apply
  (section 5). A Helicopter can't take ground alone (section 22), so it's
  rejected against an empty region.
- DEFENDED target: the attacking unit MOVES IN alongside the defenders and
  the region becomes "contested" — both armies co-locate, ownership does
  NOT change, and no Citizen penalty applies yet. The region detail panel
  shows the pending battle as "defenders (X) ⚔ attackers (you)". This is
  the combat move; the actual battle belongs to the Attack Phase.

The ATTACK PHASE itself is still a pass-through: the dice-based combat that
would resolve a contested region (sections 9-14, missiles section 15, hit
points, casualties, retreat) is future work. A red "resolve combat" control
on contested regions will live here once that exists. A successful
attack/contest consumes the unit's path cost in movement points and marks
it as having fought this turn, excluding it from Tactical Moves (section 17).

---

# 9. COMBAT PHASE (WITHIN THE ATTACK PHASE)

Battles are resolved one at a time.

Combat continues until:

- attacker destroyed
- defender destroyed
- attacker retreats
- defender retreats

---

# 10. COMBAT ORDER

Each combat round is a simultaneous exchange — both sides roll before
either side's casualties are removed, so a unit that will die this round
still gets its own roll first. Exact order:

1. Missile attacks
2. Attacker rolls
3. Defender rolls
4. Defender removes casualties
5. Attacker removes casualties
6. Retreat check
7. Repeat

---

# 11. DICE

Every unit rolls exactly one D6.

Hit occurs when

Roll <= Attack Value

while attacking.

Hit occurs when

Roll <= Defense Value

while defending.

Attack and Defense values are loaded from units.json.

Never hardcode values.

---

# 12. HIT POINTS

Most units are destroyed by a single hit.

Some units have more than one hit point (e.g. Super Aircraft Carrier needs
2 hits to be destroyed). Hit points are data-driven per unit, never
hardcoded.

---

# 13. CASUALTIES

Both sides roll before either side's casualties are removed (section 10)
— a unit due to be destroyed this round still gets its own roll first.

After both rolls:

Defender removes units hit by the attacker's roll.

Attacker removes units hit by the defender's roll.

Destroyed units never participate in later rounds.

---

# 14. RETREAT

No retreat is allowed during Combat Round 1.

Beginning with Combat Round 2:

Attacker may retreat.

Defender may retreat.

Retreat destination must be a friendly adjacent region.

If none exists, retreat is impossible.

---

# 15. MISSILES

A Rocket System moved into a DEFENDED enemy region during Attack Moves
(section 7) declares a missile strike there instead of a normal attack —
its own Attack value is 0, so this is the only offensive thing it can do.
It still physically occupies the region afterward like any other attacking
unit (co-located with the defenders, "contested").

Missiles resolve FIRST in the Attack Phase, before any other combat
(section 8). When that region's battle opens, the attacker chooses one
missile from their Arsenal (Reserve) to fire, if they have any — no missile
owned means the missile sub-phase is skipped entirely and the battle goes
straight to normal combat (with the Rocket System itself contributing
nothing, since its Attack is 0).

Interception: possible only if the defender has their OWN Rocket System in
the targeted region or an adjacent one. Roll <= 2 destroys the incoming
missile before it hits — remove one missile from Arsenal, nothing else
happens. No defending Rocket System in range means no interception attempt
at all.

Hit resolution: if not intercepted, the attacker rolls against the
missile's own Attack value (section 16). A hit destroys one defending unit
in the target region, chosen by the defender. Remove one missile from
Arsenal either way (fired or intercepted).

---

# 16. MISSILE TYPES

Missile A

Hits on a roll of <= 2.

Missile B

Hits on a roll of <= 4.

Attack values are loaded from units.json (section 41) — never hardcoded.
There are no nuclear missiles.

---

# 17. TACTICAL MOVES PHASE

After the Attack Phase resolves, the player may reposition units that did
NOT participate in an attack this turn ("non-combat move"). Units that
fought this turn (moved into an enemy region during Attack Moves, section 7)
cannot move at all during Tactical Moves.

Unlike Attack Moves, destinations here are restricted to territory the
player already owns — no entering enemy or neutral regions at all. Tactical
Moves is for repositioning within your own lines, not expansion (that's what
Attack Moves is for). On the map, only units that did NOT fight this turn
and still have a friendly destination are outlined in gold as movable.

Same movement allowance and terrain-connection rules as Attack Moves
(section 7): movement allowance, terrain connections, strait bridges. Naval
units are unrestricted between sea zones (sea zones have no owner).

Future work: per-unit exceptions to "fought units can't move" (e.g. Fighters
returning to a friendly region after combat, per sections 20-30) are not yet
implemented — this depends on the dice-based combat system (section 9-14),
which does not exist yet (see section 8).

---

# 18. PLACE NEW UNITS PHASE

Units purchased this turn (Buy Units Phase, section 4) leave the Reserve and
are deployed onto the map now, at a factory region the player controls.

Production capacity per region is loaded from JSON (never hardcoded).

---

# 19. COLLECT INCOME PHASE

This phase remains in the turn cycle for pacing/UI purposes, but it takes no
player action — the engine automates income collection (see below).

Income equals the sum of all controlled region values.

Factories, technologies and future bonuses may modify income.

Income is credited automatically, not manually collected here. As soon as a
player's turn ENDS (the engine advances past this phase to the next player),
the engine immediately credits the INCOMING player's treasury before their
Buy Units Phase begins. This means a player's money is always ready the
instant their turn starts — there is no manual "Collect Income" action and
no risk of forgetting to collect.

# 20. INFANTRY

Base stats come from units.json.

For an additional cost (see units.json), Infantry may be upgraded to an
Airborne variant: 3 Movement, can fly (ignores terrain connections like
Aircraft).

IMPLEMENTED as a directly-purchasable catalog entry ("infantry-airborne" in

on an already-deployed/reserved Infantry — buying it through the normal
Buy Units picker achieves the same "spend +5 more to get the airborne

# 21. TANK

If the first region in its attack path is unoccupied, a Tank may continue
into and capture a second region in the same move (capture-through).

IMPLEMENTED (units.json "captureThrough": true on tank; AttackCommand).
Naturally capped at 2 captures per turn by the Tank's own movement
allowance — no separate counter needed.

# 22. HELICOPTER

Cannot capture a region on its own.

# 26. FIGHTER

Movement 4 (can travel up to 4 regions), ignores terrain like all aircraft.
IMPLEMENTED — multi-hop air range (units.json movement: 4; RulesEngine BFS).

When transported, a Fighter can ONLY be carried by an Aircraft Carrier —
never a Land Transport or Stealth Boat (units.json "requiresCarrier": true,
matched against the transport's transportAcceptsFighters). Helicopters have
no such restriction (section 22). IMPLEMENTED (LoadUnitCommand /
getLoadableTransportTargets).

Must always end its move on a friendly region.

Cannot land on a region it just fought in this turn — it must land
elsewhere (section 17).

CURRENT IMPLEMENTATION STATUS: the "must end on friendly / can't land where
it fought" landing constraints are not implemented — they depend on the
dice-based combat system (section 17) existing first, so there's a real
"just fought in" outcome to react to. Multi-hop range itself is done.

# 27. ROCKET SYSTEM

Rocket Systems never perform normal attacks; they only launch missiles.

A Rocket System must carry (hold) a missile for at least one full turn
before it can be fired.

Rocket Systems defend their own region and adjacent friendly regions
against enemy missile attacks.

Gets +1 Attack when defending against Fighters.

CURRENT IMPLEMENTATION STATUS: not implemented — entirely dependent on the
missile system (section 15/16), which doesn't exist yet (no
FireMissileCommand, no missile inventory tracking).

# 28. LAND TRANSPORT CARRIER

Capacity: 2 land units + 1 air unit.

Example loadouts: 2x Infantry; or 1x Infantry + 1x Tank; or 1x Infantry +
1x Rocket System.

Transporting units does not reduce the carrier's own combat capability.

IMPLEMENTED (units.json transportLandCapacity 2 + transportAirCapacity 1;
LoadUnitCommand enforces per-category free slots). Land AND support units
(e.g. Rocket System) use the 2 land slots; a Helicopter uses the 1 air slot.
A Fighter CANNOT board a Land Transport (its air slot is helicopter-only —
only the Aircraft Carrier accepts Fighters, sections 26/29).

---

# 29. SUPER AIRCRAFT CARRIER

Capacity: 3 air units.

Requires 2 hits to be destroyed (section 12).

Transporting units does not reduce the carrier's own combat capability.

PARTIALLY IMPLEMENTED: the 3-air-unit capacity is done (units.json
"aircraft-carrier" transportAirCapacity 3, transportLandCapacity 0,
transportAcceptsFighters true; LoadUnitCommand enforces it — Fighters AND
Helicopters, no land units). The Aircraft Carrier is the ONLY transport a
Fighter can board (sections 26). The "2 hits to destroy" multi-hit-point
rule is still future work (needs the combat/hit-point system, section 12).

# 30. TRANSPORT (GENERAL)

Transports (Land Transport Carrier, Aircraft Carrier, Stealth Boat) are
naval vessels that sit in sea zones and move up to their Movement value in
sea zones (the Land Transport moves 2). To LOAD, the transport must be in a
sea zone that borders (SeaZone.adjacentRegionIds) the region holding the
land/air unit — the passenger boards from the coast and then rides in the
transport's sea zone, MOVING WITH IT when the transport sails (embarked
units follow the transport's location and don't spend their own movement
while carried). Loading is done by DRAGGING a unit onto the transport's sea
zone on the map; there is no movement panel, only a small utility panel for
Deploy (Place New Units) and Unload.

UNLOADING is phase-dependent and always costs the unit one movement point
(the landing is its move for the turn):
- During TACTICAL MOVES: a peaceful landing onto a friendly or empty coast
  bordering the transport's sea zone.
- During ATTACK MOVES: an amphibious ASSAULT onto a HOSTILE coast — the
  disembark IS the attack. An undefended coast is captured (with the
  section-5 by-force Citizen penalties); a defended coast becomes contested
  (both armies co-locate, ownership unchanged), exactly like a land attack
  (section 8). A Helicopter still can't take an undefended coast on its own
  (section 22). So the typical flow is "load + sail this turn, then next
  turn disembark as the unit's move — peacefully in Tactical Moves, or as an
  amphibious assault in Attack Moves."

Transported units:

Cannot attack while embarked.

Move with the transport when it sails; disembarking costs the unit one
movement point.

If the transport is destroyed, all transported units are destroyed.

CURRENT IMPLEMENTATION STATUS: loading (from an adjacent coast, typed
land/air capacity, Fighters carriers-only), boarding, sea movement carrying
cargo (embarked units' regionId follows the transport, their own movement
untouched), peaceful unloading (Tactical Moves) and amphibious-assault
unloading (Attack Moves — capture or contest) are all implemented
(LoadUnitCommand / MoveUnitCommand / UnloadUnitCommand). "Cannot attack
while embarked" holds (embarked units aren't pickable). "Destroyed
transport destroys its cargo" is not implemented — there's no
unit-destruction path yet, since dice-based combat resolution doesn't exist
(section 8).

# 31. REGION CONTROL

A region belongs to the player occupying it after combat.

If enemy units remain:

Ownership does not change.

If all defenders are destroyed:

Ownership immediately changes.

Income changes next Collect Income Phase.

Capturing by force drops the capturing player's Citizen marker by 5
(section 5). Capturing by political hacking does not.

---

# 32. CAPITALS

Every faction owns one Capital.

Loss of Capital does not immediately eliminate a player.

If a faction's Citizen marker triggers a rebellion (section 5), the rebel
army attacks the faction's own Capital region.

---

# 33. STARTING SETUP

Each faction begins the game with a fixed roster of units pre-deployed
across specific regions and adjacent sea zones, per the recommended setup.

Naval units must start in sea zones adjacent to a friendly region.

Fighters and Helicopters start in friendly land regions or airbases.

Missile A and Missile B tokens are NOT placed during setup unless a
scenario says otherwise.

The concrete per-faction, per-region starting roster is entirely
data-driven — see data/starting-deployment.json. Never hardcode it.
